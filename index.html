Why Nairobi?

The M-Pesa Revolution - Where Innovation Meets Everyday Life

Long before Silicon Valley was talking about fintech disruption, Nairobi was living it. In 2007, M-Pesa launched from this city and transformed how an entire continent thinks about money. What started as a simple mobile money transfer service became a blueprint for financial inclusion worldwide. Today, you can buy street food from a vendor in Kibera, pay for a matatu ride in rush hour traffic, or split a bill at a Westlands restaurant—all without touching cash. This wasn't just an app; it was a revolution born from understanding real user needs.


Walk through Nairobi today and you'll see why this city continues to earn its nickname as the "Silicon Savannah." From the startup hubs of Ngong Road to the innovation labs in Kilimani, there's an electric energy of builders and dreamers solving Africa's challenges with technology. Co-working spaces buzz with developers coding the next big solution, while rooftop meetings overlook a skyline that blends acacia trees with glass towers. Here, innovation isn't about disrupting for disruption's sake—it's about creating solutions that actually work for millions of people. And that's the Nairobi spirit: scrappy, ambitious, and relentlessly practical.

Carnivore Restaurant

An iconic all-you-can-eat experience featuring traditional Kenyan BBQ and exotic game meats. Swords of sizzling meat delivered to your table—this isn't dinner, it's a cultural institution.

Address:

Langata Road, Nairobi

What I Like About It

It's unapologetically Kenyan and impossible to forget. Where else can you experience traditional nyama choma culture elevated to an art form? Every visitor needs this experience at least once.

Red Hill Art Gallery & Café

Limuru's artistic escape featuring contemporary East African art alongside farm-to-table coffee. The gallery hosts emerging artists while the café serves some of the freshest brews outside the city.

Address:

Red Hill Road, Limuru

What I Like About It

The perfect escape when city life gets too loud. Sipping locally-roasted coffee while surrounded by bold African art reminds me why Nairobi's creative scene is world-class yet authentically homegrown.
